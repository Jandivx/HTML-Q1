Chichen Itza

Chichen Itza is a large pre-Columbian archaeological site built by the Maya civilization in the Yucatán Peninsula of Mexico.
was a major focal point in the northern Maya lowlands from the Late Classic (c. 600–900 AD) through the Terminal Classic (c. 800–1000 AD)
and into the early portion of the Postclassic period (c. 1000–1200 AD).

Chichen Itza is one of the most visited archaeological sites in Mexico and is a UNESCO World Heritage Site. It features a variety of architectural styles,
including the iconic El Castillo pyramid, which was used for astronomical observations and religious ceremonies.

The site is also known for its impressive ball court, cenotes (natural sinkholes), and the Temple of the Warriors.
Chichen Itza played a significant role in the history and culture of the Maya civilization, serving as a political, economic, and religious center.
